Targets and Strategic Planning

Targets

The Information Systems Study Program expects study program graduates to meet predetermined learning outcomes. Learning outcomes are abilities obtained through internalization of knowledge, attitudes, skills, competencies, and accumulated work experience. The learning outcomes of the Information Systems Study Program are prepared based on the learning outcomes of the information systems study program (S1) which have been formulated by APTIKOM. The following are the learning outcomes of the Information Systems Study Program:

CP-1: Ability to identify, formulate and problem solve the information needs of an organization;

CP-2: Ability to effectively integrate information technology-based solutions in an organization;

CP-3: Ability to apply basic concepts in planning information systems, designing information systems, building information systems, operating information systems, and evaluating information systems;
